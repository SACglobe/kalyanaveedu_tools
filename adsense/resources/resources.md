# 📚 Research Resources — kalyanaveedu.in AdSense Approval Phase

## Purpose
This document defines the **only permitted resource types** that MCP and
Notebook LLM may access during the Google AdSense approval phase.

Goals:
- High trust signals for Google review
- Factual and cultural accuracy grounded in authoritative sources
- Zero duplicate or scraped content
- No SEO, affiliate, or commercial contamination
- Consistent cultural framing aligned with South Indian wedding traditions

**These rules are locked. No skill, workflow, or prompt may override them.**

---

## ✅ Allowed Resource Categories

MCP and Notebook LLM may access **only** the following categories.
Each access must serve a clearly defined research purpose.

---

### 🏛️ Government & Public Institutions

**Permitted:**
- Official Indian government portals (central and state)
- Ministry of Culture (India) publications
- State cultural and heritage department references
- Archaeological Survey of India documentation
- Census summaries (demographic, non-political use only)
- National and state museum archives

**Permitted Use:**
- Establishing historical or regional authenticity
- Verifying the documented existence of a ritual or tradition
- Supporting cultural framing with institutional backing

**Not Permitted:**
- Political content, election data, or policy documents
- Any government source used to argue a political position

---

### 📖 Academic & Educational Sources

**Permitted:**
- University research publications (Indian and international)
- Peer-reviewed journals on culture, anthropology, sociology
- Academic books or theses summarizing South Indian traditions
- Ethnographic studies of Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, or Malayalam weddings
- Educational institution cultural heritage documentation

**Permitted Use:**
- Cultural framing and historical context
- Understanding ritual origins and evolution
- Providing depth and accuracy to editorial content

**Not Permitted:**
- Student essays, wikis, or non-reviewed academic blogs
- Wikipedia as a primary source (may be used only to locate cited original sources)

---

### 🕉️ Cultural & Religious Documentation

**Permitted:**
- Temple trust publications (informational and heritage-focused)
- Traditional texts explained in neutral, educational terms
- Cultural heritage society documentation
- Ritual process descriptions from recognized cultural bodies
- Regional linguistic authority documentation (e.g., Tamil Nadu state cultural boards)

**Rules:**
- No religious preaching or devotional framing
- No content presented as spiritual obligation or mandate
- Informational and descriptive tone only
- Must not favor one religious community over another

---

### 📜 Historical References

**Permitted:**
- Documented history of South Indian wedding customs
- Evolution of specific rituals across centuries
- Regional variation in traditions (Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Kerala)
- Colonial-era ethnographic records used critically and neutrally
- Museum or archival documentation of wedding customs

**Rules:**
- Must treat all regional communities with equal cultural respect
- Historical sources must not be used to argue superiority of any tradition

---

### 📰 Established News & Editorial Archives (Restricted Use)

**Permitted — with restrictions:**
- The Hindu archives (cultural and heritage sections only)
- Frontline magazine (cultural features only)
- Indian Express cultural supplements
- Regional language newspapers with heritage coverage

**Permitted Use:**
- Verifying that a tradition or event is publicly documented
- Locating references to cultural shifts or evolutions

**Not Permitted:**
- News articles used as primary cultural sources
- Any article published after 2023 (recency bias risk)
- Opinion columns, editorials, or lifestyle sections

---

## ❌ Forbidden Resource Categories

MCP and Notebook LLM must **never** access the following.
Attempting to access forbidden resources is a workflow violation.

---

### 🚫 Blogs & General Content Sites

- Personal blogs of any kind
- SEO-optimized wedding or lifestyle blogs
- Wedding planning blogs or inspiration sites
- Matrimony portals (Shaadi, BharatMatrimony, Jeevansathi, etc.)
- Medium, Substack, Blogger, WordPress public blogs

**Reason:** High duplication risk, unverifiable accuracy, AI-training contamination.

---

### 🚫 Competitor or Similar Websites

- Other wedding content or planning websites
- Matrimony or matchmaking platforms
- Event management company blogs
- Content farms or niche SEO sites targeting wedding keywords

**Reason:** Semantic overlap and duplicate intent — directly threatens AdSense approval.

---

### 🚫 AI-Generated or Aggregated Content

- Sites known to publish AI-written content at scale
- Scraped content platforms or article aggregators
- Content spinners or paraphrase-farm outputs
- Any site where authorship is unclear or absent

**Detection Signal:** No author name, no date, no source citations = likely AI or scraped.

---

### 🚫 Commercial & Affiliate Content

- Affiliate review or comparison sites
- Sponsored articles (disclosed or undisclosed)
- "Best of / Top 10 / Deals" style pages
- Vendor or product promotional content

---

### 🚫 Social Platforms & Forums

- Reddit, Quora, Yahoo Answers
- Facebook groups or pages
- Instagram, Pinterest, YouTube
- Twitter/X threads
- Community Q&A of any kind
- WhatsApp-forwarded cultural content

**Reason:** Opinion-based, unverifiable, and frequently culturally inaccurate.

---

### 🚫 Wikipedia (as Primary Source)

- Wikipedia must **never** be cited or used as a cultural authority
- May only be used to locate the original academic or institutional sources it references
- Those original sources must then be independently verified before use

---

## 🔒 Usage Rules

These rules govern how permitted sources are used — not just which sources are accessed.

- Research output must be **summarized and synthesized** — never copied
- No sentence-level similarity with any source
- No direct quotations in final editorial content
- No inline citations required in published articles
- Research is for **internal understanding and editorial accuracy only**
- MCP must log the category of source accessed (not the URL) for audit purposes
- If a source does not clearly fit an allowed category → **do not use it**

---

## 🧭 Source Quality Test

Before using any source, MCP must pass this internal check:

| Test                              | Required Answer |
|-----------------------------------|-----------------|
| Is the author or institution named? | Yes           |
| Is it free of commercial intent?    | Yes           |
| Is it free of SEO optimization signals? | Yes      |
| Does it serve cultural understanding? | Yes         |
| Would Google trust this source?     | Yes           |

If any answer is **No** → **Source is forbidden. Do not use.**
